# Persona: The Team Leader Innovator

## Profile

- **Name**: Taylor Kim
- **Age**: 38
- **Location**: Beverly Hills
- **Experience**: 12+ years, marketing background
- **Market**: $10M–$50M properties
- **Practice**: Leads 10-person team at major brokerage

## Context

Taylor's team produces inconsistent market updates — agents hedge predictions to avoid risk, diluting the firm's advisory image. HNW clients expect institutional-grade research, but compiling data from 52 sources is resource-intensive. Taylor needs a scalable tool to standardize intelligence across the team.

## Vocabulary

- Thinks in terms of "brand" and "thought leadership"
- Uses "scalable" and "standardize" — operations-minded
- Calls the report a "publication" — treats it as a brand asset
- References "internal methodology" and "team training"
- Says "client retention" and "advisory product"

## Patience Level

**Low-Medium** — Taylor is busy leading a team. Wants the tool to be efficient. Setup should be quick, customization should be templated, and output should be consistent across team members. Won't tolerate a clunky workflow.

## Frustrations

- Inconsistent quality across team members' market updates
- Agents who hedge instead of taking positions
- Resource-intensive data compilation from dozens of sources
- No way to train agents on transparent, position-taking analysis
- Reports that look like flyers instead of publications

## Success Metric

"Can every agent on my team produce a report that positions our firm as thought leaders?"

## Why They Buy

The LMIR is a scalable tool to standardize intelligence across the team. Proprietary analytics (Segment Bifurcation Monitor, confidence ratings) train agents on transparent analysis. The polished output positions the firm as thought leaders.

## Key Report Sections They Value

- Executive Summary & Market Analysis Matrix (team standardization)
- Comparative Market Analysis (competitive positioning)
- Strategic Summary with Confidence Levels (training tool)
- Report branding/customization (firm identity)
