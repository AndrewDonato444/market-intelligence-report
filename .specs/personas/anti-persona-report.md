# Anti-Persona: The Report Reader We're NOT Writing For

## Who This Is

### The Academic Analyst
A researcher, appraiser, or data scientist who wants exhaustive methodology, raw data tables, statistical significance tests, and peer-reviewed rigor. They'd want the report to include confidence intervals, regression outputs, and footnoted sources for every claim.

### The Casual Browser
A consumer or first-time homebuyer browsing Zillow who wants simple answers: "is now a good time to buy?" They'd want the report to be a 2-page infographic with a traffic light rating.

### The Compliance Officer
Someone who needs the report to be legally defensible, hedged to avoid liability, and stripped of any opinion or stance. They'd want every forecast qualified with "past performance does not guarantee future results."

## Why They're Out of Scope

| Anti-Persona | What they'd need | How it would hurt the report reader |
|-------------|-----------------|--------------------------------------|
| Academic Analyst | Raw data tables, statistical tests, exhaustive citations | Kills readability. Our reader skips anything that looks like a textbook. |
| Casual Browser | Simplified summaries, no jargon, 2-page format | Strips the density and specificity that makes the report worth paying for. |
| Compliance Officer | Hedged language, disclaimers on every insight, no forecasts | Destroys conviction. Our reader needs stances, not caveats. |

## What to Say If Requested

- **"Add raw data tables"** → "We include methodology transparency in Section 9 and specific data points throughout. Raw data exports are a separate product."
- **"Make it simpler for consumers"** → "This report is written for luxury real estate professionals. Consumer-facing summaries are a different deliverable."
- **"Remove the forecasts / add more disclaimers"** → "The Forward Look section includes confidence ranges and explicit assumptions. A standard disclaimer is included in Section 9. The editorial voice is intentionally conviction-forward — that's the value proposition."
- **"Make it shorter"** → "We can reduce page count by tightening persona briefs, but every core section must earn its place. The goal is density, not brevity."
