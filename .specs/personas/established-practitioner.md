# Persona: The Established Solo Practitioner

## Profile

- **Name**: Jordan Ellis
- **Age**: 45
- **Location**: Aspen
- **Experience**: 15 years in ultra-luxury
- **Market**: $20M+ mountain estates
- **Practice**: Solo agent, boutique firm, referral-driven

## Context

Jordan has a solid client base but relies on anecdotal insights from past deals. As markets become more volatile, clients — often wealth managers and executives — demand conviction-backed forecasts, not flyers. Jordan's reports feel outdated compared to institutional analyses from firms like John Burns Research.

## Vocabulary

- Uses "advisory" and "consultation" — never "sales call"
- Talks about "conviction" and "positioning" — wants to take a stance
- Calls report sections "the brief" or "the outlook"
- References "wealth managers" and "family offices" as client types
- Says "market timing" and "risk-adjusted" naturally

## Patience Level

**High** — Jordan is deliberate and will invest in getting the report right. Quality over speed. Will read every section and edit narratives. Values editorial voice.

## Frustrations

- Anecdotal insights no longer cutting it with sophisticated clients
- Can't compete with institutional research firms on data depth
- Building a data team as a solo agent is impractical
- Routine updates feel low-value compared to what clients expect
- Qualitative observations lack the authority of quantified trends

## Success Metric

"Did my client make a decision based on my analysis — and was it the right call?"

## Why They Buy

The LMIR's editorial perspective and decision architecture let Jordan evolve into an advisory role. The Market Insights Index and Risk Management dimensions quantify what Jordan previously described qualitatively, enhancing trust without building a data team.

## Key Report Sections They Value

- Forward Outlook & Strategic Forecasts (the advisory core)
- Key Drivers / Five Strategic Themes (narrative depth)
- Market Analysis Matrix with intelligence ratings (conviction tool)
- Methodology & Sources (credibility with institutional clients)
